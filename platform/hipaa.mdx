HIPAA compliance in Futurum is not a checkbox — it's how the product is architected. Every conversation, every note, every patient interaction is protected with end-to-end encryption, strict access controls, and audit logging by default. You don't need to configure HIPAA compliance; it's already on.

## What HIPAA Compliance Means in Futurum

Futurum enforces every required HIPAA technical safeguard at the infrastructure level. The following protections are active on every account, for every patient interaction, from day one.

- All protected health information (PHI) is encrypted at rest and in transit
- Access to patient data is controlled by role — staff see only what they need to see
- Every access event is logged automatically with user identity and timestamp
- EHR integrations use encrypted transmission channels
- Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) are available for covered entities

## Covered Data Types

HIPAA protections apply to all patient data that flows through the Futurum platform, including:

- Patient conversations and transcripts
- Clinical notes and SOAP documentation
- Billing records and medical codes
- Wearable and device health data
- Pre-assessment and intake information

## Audit Trails

Every interaction on the Futurum platform generates an automatic, tamper-evident audit log. You don't need to enable logging — it runs continuously in the background.

- Every patient interaction generates an automatic audit log
- Logs are available for review at any time from your dashboard
- Each log entry includes: action type, user, timestamp, and affected record
